Nipples tips
Tip 1: Nipple types

Nipple types: can be flat, protruding, inverted or unclassified (multiple or divided), it is possible to have one breast with one type from the other breast in the same individual.

Tip 2: Inverted nipples

Inverted nipples, are sunk in, instead of protruding out, the function the same as the protruded ones and may be sunken all the time or come in and out. Breast-feeding often brings them out. Stimulation and cold may do the same. Surgery can convert them to protrusion. Occasionally one nipple may be divided into two makes breast-feeding a challenge, as they may not fit in the little baby’s mouth. The average nipple height is about 1cm and sits in the middle or the areola or the areola (darker skin) surrounds it measuring on average 2-finger breadth in diameter.

Tip 3: Breast-feeding mother

Nipple pain for a breast-feeding mother may be a sign or a result of poor positioning during breast-feeding. For women not breast-feeding, nipple pain may be related to your monthly period cycle, hormone changes, skin irritation, skin allergies or friction from a bra as may happen with sports bras. A nipple discharge is not normal, it should be checked by a doctor. Other nipple physical abnormalities: Extra nipples below the ‘main’ breast or up in the or near the armpit or the absence of them altogether.

Tip 4:Bumps on the areola

Bumps on the areola are called Montgomery glands they serve to lubricate the nipple and they are a normal part of the anatomy.
